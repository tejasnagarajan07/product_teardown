Definition

What Is a Product Teardown?

A product teardown is a structured analysis that dismantles a product or a service to understand its design decisions, user experience, business model, and technical choices — revealing why it succeeds or fails.

Analyze

Examine every layer of a product / service: UX flows, features, onboarding, and edge cases

Understand

Uncover the 'why' behind design decisions, trade-offs, and product strategies

Apply

Extract lessons and patterns to improve your own product thinking and execution

Leadership

Why This Is Critical for
Techno-Functional Leaders

As techno-functional leaders, you sit at the intersection of business and engineering. Product teardowns are your bridge — helping you speak both languages fluently and lead with confidence.

Functional Lens
What teardowns teach you about the product side:
  • Understand user needs, workflows, and pain points from a structured lens
  • Evaluate UX patterns, onboarding flows, and feature trade-offs
  • Inform product roadmap decisions with real-world benchmarks
  • Identify gaps your product can fill vs. market gaps
Technical Lens
What teardowns unlock on the engineering side:
  • Decode technical stack choices and understand their architectural implications
  • Participate meaningfully in tech discussions — ask the right questions
  • Evaluate build vs. buy decisions with real product context
  • Make informed technical trade-off decisions grounded in product reality
Value to Presidio

The Gap We're Addressing

Solution Owners sit at the most critical intersection in any engagement — between what the client wants, what the business needs, and what engineering can build. Yet most SO development focuses on delivery execution: sprint hygiene, stakeholder management, requirement gathering.

What's missing is product judgment — the ability to look at a solution and ask not just "is it built right?" but "is it the right thing to build?"

Product teardowns are how we build that muscle — deliberately, as a practice-wide habit.

1

Client Credibility

When an SO walks into a client conversation having studied best-in-class solutions in that domain, the dynamic shifts. Instead of responding to requirements, they're shaping them.

Clients don't just want a team that executes, they want a partner who brings informed opinions. Teardowns give our SOs the vocabulary and the evidence to do that.

From order-taker to trusted advisor — in the same role, with the same team.
2

Solution Quality

Teardowns train SOs to benchmark what they're building against what's possible — not just what's been asked for.

When an SO has studied how three different products handle the same workflow, they make better decisions on edge cases, UX patterns, and architecture trade-offs. They catch gaps before they become rework. They push back on requirements that would lead to poor outcomes — with evidence, not just instinct.

Better inputs into engineering = fewer course corrections mid-delivery.
3

Career Growth for SOs

The path from Solution Owner to Manager, Engagement Director, or even CPO requires one thing more than any other: the ability to think at the product level, not just the project level.

Teardowns are a structured way to build that thinking. SOs who practice teardowns regularly develop stronger product narratives, sharper prioritisation instincts, and the confidence to lead solution design — not just oversee it.

Bigger Picture

What It Means for Presidio

Product teardowns don't just make better SOs — they create a compounding business advantage for the practice across positioning, billing, and new revenue opportunities.

Better Project Positioning

SOs with teardown practice walk into client conversations with benchmarks, not just slides. They position Presidio as the partner who understands the client's competitive landscape — not just one who delivers against a spec. This shifts how clients perceive the engagement from day one.

A New Revenue Line — Business Assessments

Just as we offer Architectural Assessments today, teardown-trained SOs can independently lead Business Assessments — evaluating a client's product or service against best-in-class benchmarks.

  • Assess the client's current product or service delivery against market leaders
  • Identify gaps in UX, feature design, business model, or GTM strategy
  • Deliver a structured findings report with prioritised recommendations
  • Position Presidio as both a strategic advisor and implementation partner

Higher Billing Possibilities

As technical and product expertise grows, SOs move from execution roles into advisory roles — commanding higher billing rates. A teardown-trained SO isn't just a delivery resource, they're a strategic asset. Clients pay more for partners who shape solutions, not just build them.

Architectural Assessment → Business Assessment → Full Advisory Engagement

Workshop

Product Teardown Workshop — Structure

10 min

Step 1 — Pick the Product / Service

Choose a product/service everyone can access or are aware of.

15 min

Step 2 — Solo Exploration

Each participant explores independently using the 5-Lens Framework. No discussion yet — capture raw observations.

20 min

Step 3 — Group Share-Out

Round-robin sharing of key findings per lens. Document everything on a shared whiteboard or Miro.

10 min

Step 4 — Synthesis

As a team, identify top 3 lessons and 1 feature/idea worth stealing or avoiding in your own product / service.

5 min

Step 5 — Action Items

Assign 1 owner per insight to explore further. Share findings async with the wider team.
